Why World Cup 2026 Is Different

Every World Cup brings roaming charge headaches. But 2026 is a different beast entirely.

For the first time in history, the tournament is being hosted across three countries simultaneously — the United States, Canada and Mexico. With 16 host cities spread across an entire continent, fans following their team through the group stages and into the knockouts could find themselves crossing two international borders.

Every time you cross a border, your roaming situation changes. A UK SIM that gives you a reasonable daily rate in the USA might charge you completely different rates the moment you step into Canada or Mexico. For fans doing the full tournament experience, unexpected roaming bills can run into hundreds of pounds.

⚠️ UK networks typically charge £5–£15 per day for data roaming in the USA. A two-week World Cup trip could cost £70–£210 in roaming fees alone — before you've bought a single drink.

What Is a Travel eSIM?

An eSIM is a digital SIM card built directly into your phone. Instead of swapping out a physical card, you scan a QR code and your new data plan activates instantly — no queuing at airport kiosks, no hunting for a local SIM shop in an unfamiliar city at midnight after a six-hour flight.

For World Cup 2026 travellers, the advantages are significant. You sort everything at home before you leave, your eSIM activates the moment you land, and you keep your existing UK number active for calls and texts throughout. Your data just works.

Is My Phone Compatible?

The vast majority of smartphones released from 2019 onwards support eSIM. Here's a quick guide:

  • iPhone — XS and later (all models from 2018 onwards)
  • Samsung — Galaxy S20 and later, plus most recent A-series
  • Google Pixel — Pixel 3 and later
  • Other Android — most flagships from 2020 onwards

To check: go to Settings → Mobile Data → Add eSIM (iPhone) or Settings → Connections → SIM Manager → Add eSIM (Android). If you see the option, you're good to go.

Your phone must also be carrier-unlocked. If you bought it outright from Apple, Samsung or a retailer, it almost certainly is. If you bought it on contract directly from a network, check with them first.

Top Tips for World Cup 2026 Fans

  • Buy your eSIM before you fly — don't rely on airport Wi-Fi to set it up at the gate
  • Download offline maps — backup for each host city is always smart in an unfamiliar country
  • Check border crossing requirements — travelling between USA, Canada and Mexico requires a valid passport and possibly a visa or ETA depending on your nationality

  • Keep your UK number active — your eSIM handles data while your regular SIM handles calls. You don't miss a thing
