Do I Need an eSIM for World Cup Games in Canada & Mexico?

Short answer: yes. The moment you cross the border, your US plan stops working and roaming charges kick in. Here's how to avoid them.

Get Your eSIM Now ⚽

The 2026 World Cup is coming home to North America — and for most American fans, the US games are the easy part. Your phone works fine, you know how everything works, and data is cheap.

But what about the games in Toronto, Vancouver, Mexico City, Guadalajara or Monterrey? The moment your plane touches down outside the US, your carrier starts charging you international roaming rates — and they are not cheap.

Most US carriers charge $10-$15 per day for international data roaming in Canada and Mexico. A week following your team across the border could cost you $70-$105 in data fees alone.

Does My US Plan Cover Canada and Mexico?

Some US plans — particularly T-Mobile and certain AT&T plans — include limited data in Canada and Mexico. But there's a catch: it's usually throttled to 2G speeds, which is practically useless for maps, streaming or social media in a busy stadium environment.

Even if your plan includes some coverage, you're likely to hit data limits quickly and then face overage charges or be left with an unusable connection at the worst possible moment.

Will It Work Alongside My US SIM?

Yes — most modern phones support dual SIM, meaning you can keep your US number active for calls and texts while using the travel eSIM for data. You won't miss any calls from home while you're at the game.

Is My Phone Compatible?

If you have an iPhone XS or newer, a Samsung Galaxy S20 or newer, or a Google Pixel 3 or newer, your phone supports eSIM. Your device must be carrier-unlocked. If you bought it outright or directly from Apple, it almost certainly is. If you're not sure, go to Settings → General → About → look for Carrier Lock. If it says "No SIM Restrictions" you're good to go.
